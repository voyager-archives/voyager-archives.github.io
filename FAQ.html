Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Space Simulation Center?

A Space Simulation Center, or the Space Center as it is commonly called, is any one of futuristic spaceship flight simulators created by various companies. These simulators, built in real life, allow you to take on the roles of a starship crew, and fly out to negotiate with aliens and battle with pirates.


How did it all start? (Who started it?)

The Space Center opened in 1990 at Central Elementary School in Pleasant Grove, Utah. It's creator and director, Victor Williamson, thought of the idea while taking his students in Young Astronaut Club to a conference in Japan. Through several years of effort, writing letters for grants, and receiving permission from School Administrators, he was able to start construction. An addition to the school was built, and the Space Center began with The USS Voyager as its flagship


What is the Space Center's purpose? / Why is it here?

The Space Center originally opened with the sole intent of educating students in STEM concepts. It has since added to its curriculum social studies, leadship, and team building. Flights simulations for entertainment occur as well.

How does it keep going?

The Space Center touches the lives of those who attend. Many times it completely changes their life. It continues to thrive by those people who are dedicated to spreading this experience to others.

  • Volunteer Acting

Where is the Space Center Located?

The majority of space centers are located in Utah County, Utah. However, there are a few in Pennsylvania, and even Venezuela!


When will it come near me?

InfiniD Learning is striving to bring a space simulator to a school near you, however no official plans have been announced for where or when space centers will be built. If you're interested in bringing the Space Center experience to your community, Contact them for more information.


Can I make a Space Center?

Absolutely! You can go it on your own using the DIY Instrutions, or get in contact with either InfiniD Learning, Dreamflight, or Telos


What can the a Space Center do for me / my child / my business / my school?

Each space center is unique in the programs and classes that each offer. Some offer stellar private and corporate team-building flights, others over excellent classes teaching how to act, engineer, design, and write. To know more about what a center near you provides, reach out to them to see what they offer.
